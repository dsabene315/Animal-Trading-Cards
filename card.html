Spinosaurus

Spinosaurus roughly translates to “spine lizard.” This name comes from the large spines creating a “fin” or “sail” down their back.

  • Environment: Semi-aquatic
  • Diet: Carnivorous
  • Size: 15 meters at 6 metric tons
  • Time Period: Late Cretaceous

Considered one of the largest meat-eating dinosaurs of all time, the Spinosaurus was a dominant carnivore of the Late Cretaceous period. Spinosaurus inhabited northern Africa in what is now Egypt.
